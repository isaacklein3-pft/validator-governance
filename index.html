Section 01

Purpose and Scope

This specification defines how the Superstructure founding validator cohort coordinates governance participation, network upgrades, and operational communication while maintaining the zero-custody design and segregation of duties established in M2. It is the final M3 deliverable — the one that answers a question the architecture specs deliberately deferred: now that operators, institutions, and middleware are all defined and deployed, how do they actually make decisions together?

The governing tension is this: effective decentralized governance requires coordinated action across multiple independent parties. But governance votes on Post Fiat are root-gated actions — they require institutional signing ceremonies. Deliberation can happen anywhere. Execution cannot happen without the institution. This model defines the structures that make deliberation efficient while keeping execution custody-safe.

This document covers four things: the communication channels and operational cadence for the founding cohort; the coordination mechanics for network upgrades, routine proposals, and emergency patches; the role separation between institutional allocators, node operators, and the orchestration layer during governance events; and the dispute resolution and escalation pathways when cohort participants disagree or fail to act.

Design Decision — Why Deliberation and Execution Are Structurally Separated A simpler model would have middleware aggregate votes and execute approved proposals automatically. We rejected this because governance votes on Post Fiat are root-gated actions — they require institutional signing ceremonies. Giving middleware execution authority over governance outcomes would collapse the custody boundary that M2 was built to protect. The tradeoff is that execution speed is bounded by the slowest participating institution's signing ceremony window. That cost is acceptable: governance events are not latency-sensitive the way routing failover is, and institutional authorization on governance outcomes is a feature, not a bottleneck.
Section 02

Governance Role in the Three-Layer Architecture

Governance coordination spans all three layers, but with strictly defined roles. Deliberation happens across layers. Execution requires Layer 1.

Layer 1 — Institution

Deliberates. Votes. Signs.

  • Participates in proposal discussion via governance forum
  • Casts binding governance votes via signing ceremony
  • Authorizes upgrade execution by signing upgrade payload
  • Escalates disputes to Superstructure Governance Council
  • Cannot be compelled to sign by middleware or operators
Layer 2 — Operator

Advises. Implements. Reports.

  • Submits technical proposals via governance forum
  • Provides technical assessment of upgrade feasibility
  • Implements approved upgrades on operator infrastructure
  • Cannot cast binding governance votes
  • Cannot execute root-gated actions
Layer 3 — Middleware

Coordinates. Constructs. Broadcasts.

  • Publishes proposals to all cohort participants
  • Constructs unsigned governance payloads
  • Aggregates signed votes and checks quorum
  • Broadcasts approved execution transactions
  • Cannot vote, approve, or execute without institutional signatures
The Governance Custody Guarantee A governance proposal that reaches quorum does not execute automatically. Middleware checks quorum and constructs the execution payload. Execution requires a threshold of participating institutions to sign that payload via signing ceremony. The orchestration layer cannot execute any governance outcome unilaterally — not upgrades, not parameter changes, not emergency patches. Every execution is institutionally authorized.
Section 03

Assumption Registry and Open Questions

Confirmed Assumptions

ASSUMPTION-01 Confirmed

Governance votes on Post Fiat are root-gated actions. They require institutional signing ceremonies. Middleware constructs the unsigned payload. The institution signs inside its custody environment. Middleware broadcasts the signed transaction. This is the same ceremony flow defined in M2 and M3 — governance is not a special case.

ASSUMPTION-02 Confirmed

The founding cohort operates under a supermajority threshold model: routine proposals require 67% of participating institutions to approve. Emergency patches require 51%. Constitutional changes require 80%. These thresholds are encoded in the institution's allocation policy and enforced by middleware quorum checking.

ASSUMPTION-03 Confirmed

Maker/Checker/Reviewer role separation applies to governance signing as it does to all root-gated actions. An institution cannot have the same individual prepare and sign a governance payload. The Reviewer role is required for governance votes because they are classified as high-value actions equivalent to key rotation in their network impact.

Open Assumptions

ASSUMPTION-04 Open

The founding cohort size is assumed to be 5–15 institutional participants for quorum calculation purposes. If the cohort size changes materially, quorum thresholds should be revisited. This spec uses a minimum quorum of 3 participating institutions to prevent two parties from pushing through governance outcomes over a temporarily offline third.

Open Questions — Needs Confirmation from Post Fiat Core

UNKNOWN-01 — On-Chain Governance Transaction Type

What is the specific on-chain transaction type for casting a governance vote on Post Fiat? Is it a dedicated governance opcode, a metadata field on a standard transaction, or a UNL amendment mechanism? Until confirmed, this spec uses GOVERNANCE_VOTE as the placeholder actionClass in signing ceremony envelopes.

UNKNOWN-02 — Emergency Patch Authority

Does Post Fiat core retain unilateral authority to push emergency patches to the network independent of validator governance? If so, the emergency patch coordination process in Step 2 covers cohort coordination around a patch already in progress — not cohort authorization of the patch itself.

UNKNOWN-03 — Quorum Counting Mechanism

Is quorum calculated by number of participating institutions, by validator stake weight, or by Verified Inference score weight? This spec defaults to institution count (one institution, one vote) pending confirmation of the Post Fiat governance model.

Step 01

Communication Channels and Operational Cadence

The founding cohort operates across three communication tiers: asynchronous governance deliberation, synchronous operational coordination, and emergency response. Each tier has defined participants, response time expectations, and escalation paths. No governance decision is made in a single channel — deliberation and execution are always in separate tiers.

Communication Channels

Tier 1 — Governance Forum

Async Deliberation

The primary venue for proposal submission, technical discussion, and pre-vote deliberation. All proposals must spend a minimum of 5 days in the governance forum before a vote is called. Participants: all cohort institutions and operators. Middleware publishes proposal summaries automatically when a new proposal is submitted. The forum record is the authoritative deliberation log — it is the evidence that a proposal was adequately discussed before execution.

Tier 2 — Operational Coordination Channel

Sync Operational Updates

A dedicated channel for operational announcements, telemetry alerts, SLA status updates, and planned maintenance notices. Not a governance venue — no binding decisions made here. Response time expectation: acknowledgment within 4 hours for operational alerts, within 15 minutes for degraded validator alerts. Participants: operators and middleware operations. Institutions receive automated summaries from this channel, not raw channel access.

Tier 3 — Emergency Response Channel

Critical Incident Coordination

Activated only for Severity 1 incidents: active network partition, signing ceremony compromise, coordinated operator failure, or emergency patch deployment. Response time expectation: acknowledgment within 15 minutes of activation. All three layers participate. Middleware activates the channel automatically when GOVERNANCE_EMERGENCY_ACTIVATED fires. Tier 3 channel records are classified as audit evidence for any emergency action taken.

Tier 4 — Governance Council

Dispute Resolution and Deadlock Breaking

A standing body of 3–5 independent members with no direct stake in any cohort operator or institution. Convened only when escalated disputes cannot be resolved through standard channels or when the cohort reaches governance deadlock. Decisions are advisory for routine disputes and binding for deadlock resolution. See Step 4 for full escalation pathway.

Operational Cadence

Weekly
Cohort Operational Review — Middleware publishes weekly validator health summary: CRS scores, uptime rates, any failover events from the prior week, and open action items. Operators acknowledge within 24 hours. No institution action required unless an alert threshold is breached.
Monthly
Governance Status Review — Open proposals reviewed, voting timelines confirmed, and any pending operator remediation windows checked. Institutions with outstanding signing ceremony obligations from the prior month are notified. Quorum health checked — if fewer than the minimum 3 institutions are actively participating, Superstructure operations escalates.
Quarterly
Cohort Composition Review — Operator whitelist diversity audit, institution participation rate review, and Verified Inference score trend analysis. Any operator below the 30-day CRS trailing average threshold of 80 is flagged for performance review. Institutions that have missed more than 2 governance votes in the quarter are contacted for participation confirmation.
As Needed
Emergency Governance Session — Convened when a Severity 1 incident requires governance authorization within the standard deliberation window. Minimum notice: 1 hour. Quorum check: 51% of participating institutions must acknowledge within 15 minutes for the session to proceed.

Step 02

Governance Coordination for Network Upgrades and Emergency Patches

Standard Upgrade Process

  • 1

    Proposal Submission

    Any cohort participant (institution or operator) submits a proposal to the governance forum. Middleware automatically publishes the proposal to all participants and starts the 5-day deliberation clock. Proposal must include: description, technical specification, expected network impact, rollback plan if the upgrade fails, and estimated operator implementation window.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_PROPOSAL_SUBMITTED
    Fail: ERR_PROPOSAL_INCOMPLETE — missing required fields
    Rollback: Submitter notified of missing fields; proposal held in DRAFT state until complete
  • 2

    Deliberation Period (minimum 5 days)

    Proposal open for discussion in governance forum. Operators provide technical feasibility assessment within 48 hours of submission. Institutions review and may request clarification. Middleware tracks deliberation activity — a proposal with zero comments after 48 hours triggers a GOVERNANCE_PROPOSAL_INACTIVE alert to the submitter.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_DELIBERATION_ACTIVE
    Fail: GOVERNANCE_PROPOSAL_INACTIVE — no engagement after 48 hours
  • 3

    Vote Call

    After minimum 5-day deliberation, any institution may call a vote. Middleware confirms the deliberation period has elapsed and issues signing ceremony envelopes to all participating institutions. Each envelope contains the proposal hash, actionClass GOVERNANCE_VOTE, vote direction (approve/reject), and a 7-day expiry. The 120-second ceremony freshness window applies per individual signing — institutions do not all sign simultaneously.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_VOTE_CALLED
    Fail: ERR_DELIBERATION_PERIOD_NOT_ELAPSED
  • 4

    Vote Collection (7-day window)

    Institutions cast votes via signing ceremony. Maker prepares the vote payload. Checker verifies the proposal hash matches the deliberation forum record before signing — this is the explicit control that prevents middleware from substituting a different proposal for the one institutions discussed. Reviewer authorizes. Signed vote transmitted to middleware.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_VOTE_RECEIVED
    Fail: ERR_VOTE_PAYLOAD_HASH_MISMATCH — proposal hash in envelope doesn't match forum record
    Rollback: Institution rejects ceremony; middleware investigates envelope construction; escalate to Governance Council if mismatch is unexplained
  • 5

    Quorum Check and Execution Authorization

    When the vote window closes or quorum is reached early, middleware tallies results. If quorum approves, middleware constructs the execution payload and issues a second round of signing ceremony envelopes — this time for the execution transaction, not the vote. Execution signing uses the same Maker/Checker/Reviewer model. The upgrade does not execute until execution signatures meet the quorum threshold within the 72-hour execution window.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_QUORUM_REACHED
    Fail: ERR_QUORUM_NOT_REACHED — vote window expired without sufficient approvals
    Rollback: Proposal returned to DELIBERATION state; submitter may revise and resubmit
  • 6

    Execution and Confirmation

    Middleware broadcasts the execution transaction once execution quorum is met. Operators implement the upgrade on their infrastructure within the operator implementation window specified in the proposal. Middleware monitors for GOVERNANCE_UPGRADE_CONFIRMED on-chain. If confirmation does not arrive within 10 ledger closes, middleware alerts all participants before any retry.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_UPGRADE_EXECUTED
    Event: GOVERNANCE_UPGRADE_CONFIRMED
    Fail: ERR_UPGRADE_TX_NOT_CONFIRMED — no on-chain confirmation within 10 ledger closes
    Rollback: Alert all participants; do not retry without fresh institution authorization; escalate if retry is needed
Design Decision — Two-Round Signing (Vote + Execution) vs Single-Round A single-round model would have institutions sign an "approve and execute" payload in one ceremony. We rejected this because it collapses deliberation and execution authorization into a single act. An institution that approves a proposal in principle must separately authorize the specific execution payload — these are different judgments. The Checker's job in the execution round is to verify the execution payload matches what was approved in the vote. That verification step only exists if there are two separate signing events. The cost is two signing ceremonies per governance outcome. The benefit is an explicit human check that the thing being executed matches the thing that was voted on.

Emergency Patch Process

Emergency patches bypass the 5-day deliberation period but not the signing ceremony requirement. The deliberation period is compressed, not eliminated.

  • 1

    Emergency Declaration

    Superstructure operations or any institution declares an emergency via Tier 3 channel. Emergency must be classified: Severity 1 (active exploit or network partition) or Severity 2 (critical vulnerability, not yet exploited). Severity 1 triggers a 24-hour vote window. Severity 2 triggers a 48-hour vote window with a 1-hour deliberation minimum.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_EMERGENCY_ACTIVATED
  • 2

    Emergency Quorum Check (15 minutes)

    Middleware checks that at least 51% of participating institutions acknowledge the emergency session within 15 minutes. Institutions that do not acknowledge are marked as NON_RESPONSIVE_EMERGENCY for the session. They are not removed from the cohort but are excluded from quorum calculation for this session. The 3-institution minimum still applies — if fewer than 3 institutions acknowledge, the emergency session cannot proceed and Superstructure operations escalates to the Governance Council directly.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_EMERGENCY_QUORUM_CONFIRMED
    Fail: ERR_EMERGENCY_QUORUM_INSUFFICIENT — fewer than 3 institutions acknowledged
  • 3

    Compressed Deliberation and Vote

    Technical summary published immediately. Operators provide impact assessment within 30 minutes. Vote called as soon as the minimum deliberation period elapses (1 hour for Severity 2; immediate for Severity 1 with Governance Council authorization). Emergency vote uses 51% quorum threshold. All other signing ceremony requirements apply identically.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_EMERGENCY_VOTE_CALLED
    Event: GOVERNANCE_EMERGENCY_QUORUM_REACHED
    Fail: ERR_EMERGENCY_VOTE_EXPIRED — 24-hour window elapsed without quorum
    Rollback: Governance Council convenes; may authorize extended window or alternative remediation path
Step 03

Segregation of Duties During Governance Events

Governance events involve all three layers acting in defined roles. The segregation model is the same as M2 — Maker/Checker/Reviewer at the institutional level, with operators and middleware in supporting roles that cannot substitute for institutional authorization.

Institutional Role Separation During Governance Signing

Every governance signing ceremony follows the Maker/Checker/Reviewer model from M2. For governance votes specifically:

  • Maker prepares the vote payload, confirming the proposal hash matches the governance forum record
  • Checker independently verifies the proposal hash, vote direction, and envelope freshness before signing. The Checker must not be the same individual who prepared the payload. This is the explicit control that catches substituted or malformed payloads.
  • Reviewer authorizes the ceremony. Governance votes are classified as high-value actions — the Reviewer role cannot be waived. An institution that cannot staff a Reviewer for a governance vote must abstain from that vote rather than submit an improperly authorized signature.
Design Decision — Reviewer Required for All Governance Votes The Reviewer role could have been optional for routine governance votes (protocol parameter adjustments, minor upgrades) and required only for constitutional changes. We rejected optional Reviewer status because it creates a category system that institutions must manage — and the natural pressure is to classify votes as "routine" to skip the Reviewer overhead. Every governance vote has network-level impact. The Reviewer is not overhead; it is the control that ensures institutional authorization is genuine rather than procedural. The cost is that institutions need three available personnel for any governance participation. That staffing requirement is an explicit design goal — governance participation should require institutional capacity, not just a single person with access to a key.

What Middleware Cannot Do During Governance

Middleware has broad visibility into governance state but narrow execution authority. Specifically, middleware cannot:

  • Cast or modify votes on behalf of any institution
  • Execute an approved proposal without execution quorum signatures
  • Declare a proposal approved before quorum is reached, even if the math suggests quorum is inevitable
  • Override or extend a vote window without institution confirmation
  • Submit a dispute on behalf of any participant
  • Classify a dispute as resolved without the disputing party's acknowledgment
Step 04

Dispute Resolution and Escalation Pathways

Disputes in the founding cohort fall into three categories: technical disputes (disagreement about whether a proposed upgrade is safe or correct), process disputes (disagreement about whether governance procedures were followed), and custody disputes (a participant believes another party has violated the zero-custody model). Each has a defined resolution path.

Dispute Classification and First-Response Window

Dispute TypeExamplesFirst Response WindowInitial Resolution Path
Technical Upgrade safety disagreement; telemetry methodology dispute; operator implementation quality 48 hours Technical working group (2 operators + 1 independent technical advisor)
Process Deliberation period shortened without authorization; vote called before quorum check; execution without sufficient signatures 24 hours Superstructure operations review with governance forum record as evidence
Custody Alleged middleware access to institutional keys; signing ceremony bypassed; unauthorized execution transaction 4 hours Immediate escalation to Governance Council; all affected operations suspended pending review

Escalation Pathway

  • 1

    Dispute Filed

    Any cohort participant files a dispute via governance forum with: dispute type classification, specific allegation, supporting evidence (event logs, signing ceremony records, forum timestamps), and requested resolution. Middleware logs GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_FILED with correlation ID and notifies all cohort participants within 30 minutes.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_FILED
    Fail: ERR_DISPUTE_MISSING_EVIDENCE — filed without supporting documentation
  • 2

    Response Period

    The disputed party responds within the first-response window for the dispute type (4 hours for custody, 24 hours for process, 48 hours for technical). Response must address each allegation with evidence. Failure to respond within the window is logged as GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_NO_RESPONSE and automatically escalates to the Governance Council.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_RESPONSE_RECEIVED
    Event: GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_NO_RESPONSE — automatic escalation triggered
  • 3

    Initial Resolution Attempt

    Superstructure operations facilitates resolution between the parties using the governance forum record as the authoritative evidence source. If the dispute is resolved, both parties acknowledge resolution and middleware logs GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_RESOLVED. Resolution acknowledgment must be explicit — silence is not acknowledgment.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_RESOLVED
    Fail: GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_UNRESOLVED — parties cannot agree within 48 hours of response
  • 4

    Governance Council Escalation

    Unresolved disputes escalate to the Governance Council within 48 hours of initial filing. The Council acknowledges the dispute within 48 hours of escalation and issues a ruling within 14 days. Council rulings are binding for custody disputes and process disputes. For technical disputes, the Council ruling is advisory — the cohort votes on implementation of the recommended resolution. Council records and reasoning are published to the governance forum.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_COUNCIL_RULING_ISSUED
    Fail: ERR_COUNCIL_QUORUM_UNAVAILABLE — Council cannot convene within timeline
    Rollback: Extend ruling window by 7 days; notify all parties; suspend related governance activities pending ruling
  • 5

    Remediation or Removal

    If the Governance Council ruling requires remediation from a participant, that participant has 30 days to comply. Failure to comply results in suspension from governance participation. Suspension from governance participation means an institution's votes are excluded from quorum calculations — the cohort can continue to function. Permanent removal from the cohort requires a fresh governance vote at the 80% constitutional threshold.

    Event: GOVERNANCE_DISPUTE_REMEDIATION_COMPLETE
    Event: GOVERNANCE_PARTICIPANT_SUSPENDED
Design Decision — Advisory vs Binding Council Rulings for Technical Disputes Making Council rulings binding for technical disputes would give a 3–5 person Council the ability to force a specific technical implementation on the cohort. We rejected this because technical decisions have network-level consequences that the full cohort should own. The Council is selected for governance expertise, not necessarily deep Post Fiat protocol engineering. Their ruling sets the direction — the cohort votes on the specific implementation. For custody and process disputes, binding rulings are appropriate because those disputes are about whether rules were followed, not about technical judgment calls.

Section 09

Error Codes and Typed Failure Reasons

ERR_PROPOSAL_INCOMPLETE
Proposal Missing Required Fields

Submitted proposal is missing one or more required fields (description, technical spec, rollback plan, implementation window). Proposal held in DRAFT state until complete.

ERR_DELIBERATION_PERIOD_NOT_ELAPSED
Vote Called Before Minimum Deliberation

Institution attempted to call a vote before the 5-day minimum deliberation period elapsed. Vote call rejected. Middleware logs the attempt and the time remaining.

ERR_VOTE_PAYLOAD_HASH_MISMATCH
Vote Payload Doesn't Match Forum Record

Checker verification step found that the proposal hash in the signing ceremony envelope doesn't match the governance forum record. Institution must reject the ceremony. Middleware investigates envelope construction.

ERR_QUORUM_NOT_REACHED
Vote Window Expired Without Quorum

7-day vote window closed without sufficient approvals. Proposal returned to DELIBERATION state. Submitter may revise and resubmit.

ERR_EXECUTION_WINDOW_EXPIRED
Execution Signatures Not Collected in Time

72-hour execution window elapsed without execution quorum. All participants alerted. Fresh authorization required before any retry — the prior vote does not carry over.

ERR_UPGRADE_TX_NOT_CONFIRMED
Upgrade Transaction Not Confirmed On-Chain

No on-chain confirmation within 10 ledger closes after broadcast. All participants alerted immediately. Do not retry without fresh institution authorization.

ERR_EMERGENCY_QUORUM_INSUFFICIENT
Emergency Session Cannot Proceed

Fewer than 3 institutions acknowledged the emergency session within 15 minutes. Session cannot proceed. Superstructure operations escalates to Governance Council directly.

ERR_EMERGENCY_VOTE_EXPIRED
Emergency Vote Window Elapsed Without Quorum

24-hour emergency vote window closed without quorum. Governance Council convenes. May authorize extended window or alternative remediation path.

ERR_DISPUTE_MISSING_EVIDENCE
Dispute Filed Without Supporting Documentation

Dispute submission did not include required evidence (event logs, signing records, forum timestamps). Dispute held in PENDING state until evidence is provided.

ERR_COUNCIL_QUORUM_UNAVAILABLE
Governance Council Cannot Convene

Council cannot reach quorum within the required window. Ruling window extended by 7 days. Related governance activities suspended pending ruling. All parties notified.

ERR_REVIEWER_ROLE_ABSENT
Governance Signing Without Reviewer Authorization

Institution attempted to submit a governance vote without Reviewer authorization. Submission rejected. Institution must either staff a Reviewer or abstain from the vote.

ERR_GOVERNANCE_MINIMUM_QUORUM_BREACH
Below Absolute Minimum Quorum Floor

Fewer than 3 institutions participating in any vote type. Vote cannot proceed regardless of percentage thresholds. Cohort health alert issued to all participants.

Section 10

Governance Payload Schemas

Governance Vote Signing Ceremony Envelope

Issued by middleware when a vote is called. Institution's Checker verifies all fields before signing.

{
  "envelopeId": string,           // unique envelope identifier; used for nonce tracking
  "actionClass": string,          // "GOVERNANCE_VOTE" (placeholder — see UNKNOWN-01)
  "proposalId": string,           // unique proposal identifier from governance forum
  "proposalHash": string,         // SHA-256 of proposal document as published in forum; Checker verifies this matches
  "voteDirection": string,        // "APPROVE" | "REJECT" | "ABSTAIN"
  "institutionId": string,        // unique institution identifier from founding cohort registry
  "validatorPubKey": string,      // institution's validator public key
  "nonce": string,               // unique per-ceremony; retained 30 days for replay protection
  "issuedAt": string,            // ISO 8601 UTC; freshness window 120 seconds per individual signing
  "expiresAt": string,           // ISO 8601 UTC; vote window expiry (7 days for routine, 24h for emergency)
  "specVersion": string           // governance spec version; mismatch triggers ERR_SPEC_VERSION_MISMATCH
}

Governance Execution Payload

Constructed by middleware after vote quorum reached. Separate from vote envelope — institutions sign this in a second ceremony to authorize execution.

{
  "executionId": string,           // unique execution identifier; links to proposalId
  "proposalId": string,           // must match the voted proposal
  "proposalHash": string,         // same hash as vote envelope; Checker re-verifies before execution signing
  "actionClass": string,          // specific action being executed (e.g., "PROTOCOL_UPGRADE", "PARAMETER_CHANGE")
  "executionParams": object,      // action-specific parameters; exact fields depend on actionClass
  "voteQuorumRecord": string,     // hash of the vote tally that authorized this execution
  "executionWindowStart": string,// ISO 8601 UTC; when 72-hour execution window opened
  "executionWindowEnd": string,  // ISO 8601 UTC; window closes 72 hours after quorum reached
  "nonce": string,               // separate nonce from vote ceremony; 30-day retention
  "specVersion": string           // governance spec version
}

Dispute Filing Record

{
  "disputeId": string,            // unique dispute identifier
  "filedBy": string,             // institution or operator ID of filing party
  "disputeType": string,         // "TECHNICAL" | "PROCESS" | "CUSTODY"
  "disputedParty": string,       // institution or operator ID of disputed party
  "allegation": string,          // specific allegation in plain language
  "evidenceRefs": array,         // array of event correlation IDs, forum timestamps, or signing ceremony records
  "requestedResolution": string, // what the filing party is asking for
  "filedAt": string,             // ISO 8601 UTC; response window clock starts here
  "responseDeadline": string     // ISO 8601 UTC; 4h custody, 24h process, 48h technical
}

Runbook — Routine Proposal Lifecycle

  1. Submit proposal to governance forum with all required fields (description, technical spec, rollback plan, implementation window)
  2. Await operator technical assessment within 48 hours
  3. Allow minimum 5-day deliberation period; respond to questions in forum
  4. Any institution calls vote; middleware issues signing ceremony envelopes to all participants
  5. Each institution completes signing ceremony (Maker prepares, Checker verifies proposal hash against forum record, Reviewer authorizes) within 7-day vote window
  6. Middleware tallies votes; if quorum reached, constructs execution payload and issues execution envelopes
  7. Institutions complete execution signing ceremonies within 72 hours
  8. Middleware broadcasts; operators implement within specified window
  9. Middleware confirms on-chain; logs GOVERNANCE_UPGRADE_CONFIRMED

Runbook — Emergency Patch

  1. Declare emergency via Tier 3 channel; classify as Severity 1 or 2
  2. Middleware activates emergency session; 15-minute acknowledgment window opens
  3. Operators provide technical impact assessment within 30 minutes
  4. Minimum 1-hour deliberation (Severity 2) or immediate vote (Severity 1 with Council authorization)
  5. Emergency vote called; 24-hour window; 51% quorum threshold; Maker/Checker/Reviewer required
  6. If quorum reached: execution envelopes issued; 72-hour execution window applies equally
  7. If quorum not reached: Governance Council convenes to authorize extended window

Section 14

Zero-Custody Governance Assurance

Governance Zero-Custody Guarantee

No governance outcome — proposal approval, protocol upgrade, parameter change, emergency patch, or participant removal — executes without institutional signing ceremonies. The orchestration layer cannot approve, authorize, or execute any governance action unilaterally.

Governance votes are root-gated actions. Middleware constructs unsigned payloads. Institutions sign inside their custody environments. Middleware broadcasts signed transactions. The key never moves. The deliberation is decentralized. The authorization is not.

The two-round signing model (vote ceremony + execution ceremony) means an institution that approved a proposal in principle must separately authorize the specific execution payload. The Checker's job in the execution round is an explicit human verification that the thing being executed matches the thing that was voted on. This is not a procedural formality — it is the control that prevents middleware from executing something other than what the cohort approved.

Dispute resolution and escalation pathways are advisory and administrative processes. They do not grant any party — including the Governance Council — the ability to sign on behalf of an institution or compel an institution to participate in a signing ceremony.
